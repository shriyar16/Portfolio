About Me






Hi! My name is Shriya Rudrashetty. Currently, I am a senior at Dublin High School. After graduating high school, I plan to pursue a career in computer science. To achieve this goal, I gained exposure to the field of computer science through extracurriculars and classes at DHS. For example, choosing to take electives such as Honors Principles of Engineering and AP Computer Science Applications allowed me to build and strengthen the skills necessary to be successful in this field. Attending guest speaker events and field trips providing a glimpse into computer science in the working world, enabling me to learn more about the various career options available. In college and beyond, I am excited to continue my journey with engineering and computer science through similar classes, clubs, and personal projects. I believe computer science as a career is well suited for me because I enjoy problem solving and the chance to use my creativity. Engineering also involves the intersection between mathematics and science and their applications in the real world, which greatly interests me.

Last summer, I received the opportunity to intern at NASA Ames Research Center. Through this internship, I was able to see the intersection between environmental science, aerospace engineering, and computer science. Working with a team of 6, the internship involves researching the environmental impact of the aviation industry as well as methodologies to move towards a more sustainable future. Environmental solutions such as the usage of renewable fuel and technological solutions such as machine learning software were explored. More specifically, the impact of the new IADs software implemented in Charlotte Douglas International Airport was explored and data analysis was performed on data sets obtained from members involved with the project and public databases.
